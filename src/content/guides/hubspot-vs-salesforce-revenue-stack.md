# HubSpot vs Salesforce: Which Fits Your Revenue Stack

When choosing a CRM for your business, **[HubSpot](https://www.hubspot.com/)** and **[Salesforce](https://www.salesforce.com/)** stand out as top contenders, each with strengths tailored to different needs. Salesforce offers unmatched customization and scalability, catering to larger enterprises with complex workflows. Here’s what you need to know:

## Automation Features and Tool Connections

Revenue operations teams depend on automation to streamline repetitive tasks and standardize processes across sales and marketing. Both HubSpot and Salesforce provide robust automation tools, but they take different approaches to workflow design and tool integration.

### Workflow Automation: Simplifying Processes

HubSpot makes automation accessible to everyone on your team. Its drag-and-drop workflow builder allows users to design intricate processes without writing a single line of code. You can create workflows from scratch, use AI to build automation sequences, or select from pre-made templates tailored to common revenue operations needs. These automation tools work seamlessly across HubSpot's Sales, Marketing, and Service hubs. For example, when a lead is captured through marketing efforts, it can automatically trigger sales follow-ups, update deal stages, and notify the service team - all without manual intervention.

> "HubSpot is easy to use, easy to train people on, and easy to customize. It enables teams to excel." - Phoebe Scott, Global Director, CRM, Avison Young [\[3\]](https://www.hubspot.com/comparisons/salesforce-vs-hubspot)

Salesforce, on the other hand, offers powerful automation through tools like Process Builder, Flow Builder, and Apex code. While these options provide significant flexibility and can handle complex workflows, they often require technical expertise to implement and manage effectively. Salesforce excels at managing intricate business logic, multi-step workflows, and approval processes that span multiple departments.

HubSpot’s automation tools start at $800/month, making them a great choice for teams without dedicated IT resources. Meanwhile, Salesforce’s advanced automation capabilities, which cater to more complex needs, come with higher-tier pricing.

In March 2023, a company using HubSpot reported a 97% increase in deal close rates after implementing Sales Hub automation. These features helped streamline their sales processes and reduced the burden of manual follow-ups [\[3\]](https://www.hubspot.com/comparisons/salesforce-vs-hubspot).

For teams looking for quick setup and ease of use, HubSpot can fast-track automation deployment. However, businesses with more intricate operational needs might find Salesforce’s advanced capabilities worth the additional effort and cost.

## Data Management and Reporting Features

How your CRM handles data - storing, organizing, and turning it into actionable insights - can make or break your revenue team's effectiveness. HubSpot and Salesforce take different paths in data management, offering distinct benefits depending on how complex your data needs are and the resources you have available.

### Data Storage and Access

HubSpot structures its data around **Contacts, Companies, Deals, and Tickets**, keeping things simple and user-friendly. This setup is great for teams focusing on individual customer relationships rather than navigating intricate organizational hierarchies. HubSpot also includes automatic **deduplication for Contacts (based on email) and Companies (based on domain)**, which helps maintain clean data. However, some manual checks are still necessary before syncing [\[9\]](https://medium.com/@hubspot.lo.ai/hubspot-vs-salesforce-a-data-model-comparison-50e6fd5ed3d8).

On the other hand, Salesforce organizes its data model around the **Account**. It includes Accounts, Contacts, Leads, and Opportunities, making it ideal for larger businesses with more complex data requirements. Salesforce supports four relationship types - Lookup, Master-Detail, Many-to-Many, and Hierarchical - allowing users to map out detailed organizational structures [\[9\]](https://medium.com/@hubspot.lo.ai/hubspot-vs-salesforce-a-data-model-comparison-50e6fd5ed3d8).

Interestingly, a study conducted in September 2025 found that companies using both platforms experienced a **30% boost in lead conversion rates** after implementing bi-directional syncing. This gave marketing and sales teams better visibility into customer interactions [\[9\]](https://medium.com/@hubspot.lo.ai/hubspot-vs-salesforce-a-data-model-comparison-50e6fd5ed3d8).

> "HubSpot's streamlined approach works well for teams seeking immediate usability and clear data relationships, while Salesforce's comprehensive structure serves organizations requiring detailed customization." - R. L. Bloom, Author [\[9\]](https://medium.com/@hubspot.lo.ai/hubspot-vs-salesforce-a-data-model-comparison-50e6fd5ed3d8)

When choosing between HubSpot and Salesforce, think about whether your business needs a focus on straightforward individual interactions (HubSpot) or the ability to handle complex hierarchies (Salesforce).
